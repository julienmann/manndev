# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Small business owners, creatives, and startup founders (decision-makers, roughly 30-55) who need a website built and are evaluating whether to hire a freelancer, an agency, or a no-code platform. They're visiting to decide if Julien is credible, capable, and worth a 30-60 minute discovery call. Remote-friendly, but based out of Montréal.

## Product Purpose

A single-page portfolio and lead-generation site for Julien Mann, a solo freelance web developer. It exists to convert visitors into enquiries by demonstrating craft directly in the page itself (custom-built, no templates, GSAP-driven interactions, Lighthouse-100 target) rather than just claiming it. Success is a submitted contact form from a qualified lead.

## Brand Personality

Confident, editorial, precise. Voice is plain and direct — short sentences, no jargon, no hype language ("seriously," "actually works," "no obligation"). Personality of a craftsman who works alone and stands behind the work, not an agency selling a process. Quietly premium: dark, warm, typewriter-and-serif aesthetic rather than bright SaaS gradients.

## Anti-references

Generic SaaS landing pages (bright gradients, hero-metric blocks, stock illustrations). Bloated agency sites with layers of process jargon and team photos. Anything that reads as templated, since "no templates" is a core claim the design must visually back up.

## Design Principles

- Show, don't tell — claims about craft (clean code, custom design, performance) should be demonstrated in the page's own construction (typeset code blocks, real Lighthouse numbers, deliberate motion), not just asserted in copy.
- One person, one voice — every section should read like it came from the same hand that writes the code; no committee-speak, no department handoffs implied.
- Editorial restraint over decoration — confidence is expressed through typography, rule lines, and negative space, not gradients or stock imagery.
- Earn the scroll — interactions (process timeline, tabs, scramble text) reward attention, but never become a barrier to finding the contact form.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA baseline. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for cursor, scroll, scramble, and magnetic-button effects. Custom cursor and hover-driven interactions must degrade gracefully on touch devices (already handled via `@media(hover:none)`).
